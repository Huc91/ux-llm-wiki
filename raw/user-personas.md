# Personas

---

## Sofia Rossi

### Snapshot

- **Age:** 29
- **Location:** Milan, Italy
- **Role:** Marketing specialist at a fast-growing startup
- **Travel style:** Spontaneous, short city breaks (2-4 days), often booked 24-72 hours before departure
- **Tech behavior:** Mobile-first, compares options quickly, abandons flows that feel slow

### Behaviors

- Books last-minute trips, often during lunch breaks or late evening
- Starts with broad intent (“I need to get away this weekend”) rather than fixed plans
- Skips long research; prefers curated suggestions over exploring endless options
- Uses 2-3 apps/tabs max before deciding
- Prioritizes speed and confidence over perfect optimization

### Goals

- Complete booking in under 10 minutes
- Minimize cognitive effort (“just show me the best options”)
- Avoid surprises (hidden fees, unclear policies, awkward logistics)
- Feel confident she made a good choice fast

### Pain points

- Too many steps in checkout
- Long forms and repeated data entry
- Information overload (too many filters, too many similar options)
- Unclear total price until late in the flow
- Time-consuming planning when she just wants to go

### Needs from the product

- Fast-start flow (“Weekend from Milan” templates)
- Smart defaults and pre-filled fields
- Clear total price upfront
- Strong recommendations with “why this option” explanations
- One-tap save/share for quick confirmation with friends

### Decision triggers

- “Can I trust this option quickly?”
- “Is this the real final price?”
- “Can I finish this before my break ends?”

### Quote

> “I don’t need 50 options. I need 3 great ones and a fast checkout.”
